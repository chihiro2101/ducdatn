The 987-foot tanker Exxon Valdez runs aground on Bligh Reef some 25 miles south of Valdez, Alaska, spilling nearly 11 million gallons of North Slope crude oil into Prince William Sound.
Alaska fishermen's union spokeswoman Riki Ott says the group wants full compensation for damage from the spill.
U.S. Interior Secretary Manuel Lujan calls the spill the oil industry's ``Three Mile Island.''
Exxon calls the conservation group unfair in its criticism of the oil spill; the group calls Exxon ``a corporate pariah.''
Jan. 5: The Alaska Oil Spill Commission issues a report calling for an overhaul of the nation's oil transportation system.
